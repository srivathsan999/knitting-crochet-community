Meet the Maker: Sarah's Journey to Pattern Design

Sarah Jenkins September 15, 2024 • 6 min read

Sarah Jenkins started knitting as a way to relieve stress during college. Ten years later, she's one of the most sought-after knitwear designers in the industry. Here is her story.

From Hobby to Profession

"I never thought I could make a living doing this," Sarah admits. "It started with modifying existing patterns because they didn't quite fit right. Then I started sketching my own ideas. One day, I decided to publish a free pattern on a whim, and it went viral."

The Design Process

Sarah's process is meticulous. She spends weeks swatching and calculating sizing before she even casts on the sample. "Inclusivity is non-negotiable for me," she says. "If a pattern doesn't look good on a size 5X as well as an XS, I go back to the drawing board."

"Don't be afraid to rip it out. Mistakes are just proof that you're trying something new."

Advice for Aspiring Designers

Sarah encourages new designers to find their unique voice. "There are a million cable sweaters out there, but nobody will design one exactly like you will. Trust your instincts and write everything down!"
